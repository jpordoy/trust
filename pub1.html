Digital Health Rehabilitation Can Improve Access to Care in Spinal Cord Injury in the UK

TRUST clinicians Professor Anba Soopramanien (MD, PhD) and Shiva Jamals (MSc) proposed solution into how digital health can improve access to spinal cord injury resources has been published in the prestigious International Journal of Telerehabilitation.

Anba Soopramanien MD, PhD Shiva Jamwal MSc
|
Feb 11, 2022
|

Lack of specialist beds, inadequate finance and shortage of skilled staff make it difficult for Spinal Cord Injury Centres (SCICs) in the United Kingdom (UK) to admit all newly injured individuals. Length of stay of those admitted can be too brief. At discharge, follow-up care is sparse and inadequate. We therefore propose that specialist spinal units redefine their roles and act as catalysts to build capacity by enhancing expertise in the wider community. SCICs can devolve certain tasks locally to less specialised units with their support, training, and guidance. This Commentary further proposes that use of Digital Health Technologies, (i.e., to deploy telemedicine, telehealth, and telerehabilitation), can enhance rehabilitation opportunities.

Keywords: Digital Health; Multidisciplinary Team; Rehabilitation; Spinal Cord Injury; Telehealth; Telemedicine; Telerehabilitation.
Copyright 2020 Anbananden Soopramanien, Shiva Jamwal, Peter W. Thomas.

Medications to Prevent Infections

The authors set-forth their vision for a comprehensive web portal that will serve as a primary resource for evidence-based practice, information on guidelines, care pathways, and protocols of SCI management.
At any stage during the acute management of SCI and following discharge, rehabilitation specialists could conduct remote consultation with persons with SCI and acute care specialists via the web portal, allowing timely access to specialist input and better clinical outcomes.
The proposed portal would also provide information, advice and support to persons with SCI and their family members. The strategic use of digital health technologies has been show n to result in cost and time savings and increase positive outcomes.
